## Welcome !  

I’m Melissa, a behavioural ecologist interested in how behaviour evolves in response to social and ecological pressures. I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Psychology and Neuroscience at the University of St Andrews.

My research integrates field observations and experimental approaches to investigate how interactions among individuals arise, persist, and shape group dynamics. I am particularly interested in the trade-offs between the benefits and costs of social interactions for health and survival, and in how these relationships vary with ecological conditions. More broadly, my work aims to understand how individual-level interactions scale up to generate patterns of cooperation and competition at the group level.

While my research is primarily fundamental in nature, I have also been involved in applied research exploring links between behaviour, conservation, and animal welfare. I have worked mainly with mammals, including primates, rodents, and ungulates, but I am always keen to explore these questions across a broader range of taxa.

To learn more about my past and current research, please visit the Research page.
